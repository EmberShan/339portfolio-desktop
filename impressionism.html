Impressionism

An art movement led by Claude Monet and other Paris-based artists. It features small, thin, yet visible brush strokes. It starts first in painting then in music. One of the most conspicuous features in impressinoism paintings is the emphasis on the transient effects of light and color accurately and objectively. Often when viewing the paintings, people will notice that the paintings seem to glow.

Neo-Impressionism

Neo-impressionism utilizes Divisionism which means using dots of different colors to represent an object instead of mixing the colors together. Important neo-impressionism artists involve Georges Seurat and Paul Signac. They often only use dots of primary color and by placing the dots adjacent, they can create an illusion of light.

Post-Impressionism

Instead of optical impressions, Post-impressionism expresses emotions on themes of deeper symbolism. It emerged as a reaction against Impressinoists' emphasis for the accurate and natural depiction of light. Post-impressionism focuses more on the deeper symbolic meanings by extending the limitations of impressinoism.
