Who is Claude Monet?

self-portrait of Claude Monet by himself

Oscar-Claude Monet (14 November 1840 – 5 December 1926) was a French artist and the pioneer of Impressionism. ... He was consistent in applying Impressionism to plein air landscape painting. The word "Impressionism" is also derived from the title of one of his paintings. Monet grew up in Le Havre, Normandy. He started drawing at a very early age. While his mother supported his painting career, his father wanted him to a businessman. After his mother's death, Monet was sent to live with his aunt Marie-Jeanne Lecadre.

Impressionism

Impression, sunrise(soleil levant), Claude Monet, 1872

Impressionism related to a movement in Paris in 1860s. Impressionism received many criticism from other artists for its "unifnished" looks. ... Other artists ridiculed the movment by giving it the name "Impressionism" based on the title of one of Monet's paintings Sunrise. Impressionists aim to capture the sensory effects of a scenery instead of painting realistically. Impressionists find painting in plain air to be helpful for capturign their visual impressions. Other important Impressionist artists include Pierre Auguste Renoir, Camille Pissarro, Alfred Sisley, Berthe Morisot, Armand Guillaumin, and Frédéric Bazille.

Impressionism vs. Realism

left: Sunlight on the Coast, Winslow Homer, 1890; right: Rocky Crags at L'Estaque, Pierre-Auguste Renoir, 1882

To start simple, Impressionism paintings often contain no outlines. It is more focused on the artists' feelings about certain things like landscapes or people. ... Impressionsm may not reflect the actual colors or shapes of the objects. In the image above, the left painting is realism while the right one is impressionism. we can see that the left one has a clear outline of the sea, rocks, and waves. The foreground and background are clearly separated. However, on the right, the painting is more "mushy": colors are mixing together and the actual scenery is probably less vibrant that the painting.
